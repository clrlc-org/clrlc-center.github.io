About Center for Low-Resource Languages & Cultures (CLRLC)

Center for Low-Resource Languages & Cultures (CLRLC) is dedicated to advancing research, technology, and innovation for the thousands of languages and cultures in Africa and beyond that remain under-resourced in today’s AI ecosystem. We work to strengthen linguistic and cultural diversity by developing AI-driven tools, resources, and collaborative research projects that empower communities, scholars, and technologists around the world.

As part of our mission, we actively host workshops at major AI and NLP conferences to bring together researchers, students, and community members to exchange ideas, showcase work, and foster cross-disciplinary collaboration. We also aim to inspire and engage young people—especially those from communities with low-resource languages—to participate in research, contribute to language preservation, and explore how technology can support their linguistic and cultural heritage.

CLRLC stands for inclusion, representation, and innovation. We believe that every language and every culture deserves to be supported and celebrated in the global technological landscape.

What We Do

We conduct interdisciplinary research, develop ethical language technologies, support cultural preservation initiatives, and collaborate with communities to ensure linguistic equity globally.

Who We Are

We are a network of researchers, linguists, technologists, and community partners. The center is led by Joy Naomi along with collaborators passionate about language and cultural inclusion.
