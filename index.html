Center for Low-Resource Languages & Cultures

Advancing research, preservation, and technology for under-represented languages and cultures around the world.

About the Center

The Center for Low-Resource Languages & Cultures is dedicated to empowering communities whose linguistic and cultural identities are under-documented or digitally underrepresented. Our work focuses on research, ethical data collection, language technology development, and community-driven preservation initiatives.

What We Do

We conduct interdisciplinary research, develop ethical language technologies, support cultural preservation initiatives, and collaborate with communities to ensure their languages and cultural knowledge are represented in global digital spaces. Our activities include dataset development, workshops, training programs, and multilingual technology innovation.

Who We Are

We are a growing network of researchers, linguists, technologists, and community partners dedicated to empowering speakers of low-resource languages. The center is currently led by Joy Naomi, working alongside collaborators passionate about linguistic equity, cultural preservation, and inclusive AI.

Research & Projects

Our work spans multilingual research, cultural documentation, dataset curation, language technologies, and workshops that elevate the presence of low-resource languages in global digital ecosystems. We collaborate with linguists, AI researchers, local communities, and cultural custodians.
